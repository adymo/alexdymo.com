I have conducted two large research projects in my life. First resulted in my master thesis, second is aimed for a PhD. The projects described in more details below. But there's one important thing I must note down here. I worked in various areas of science (namely heat exchange, software engineering and project management) and came to value the interdisciplinary research. My experience in heat exchange several times inspired me when I was worked on my software engineering PhD dissertation. For example I successfully applied similarity theory (used to study convection) for software cost estimation. Also knowledge of systems theory and cybernetics helped me to formulate principles and find means of self-organization of software teams. Also I hope my recent involvement in computational linguistics will bring me new means of cognition despite of the area of research.
